1. What do we mean by ‚Äúsystemic solvency‚Äù?

Traditional solvency frameworks focus on individual entities ‚Äî banks, insurers, funds, corporates. By contrast, systemic solvency asks whether the system as a whole remains solvent and resilient under:

  • Correlated shocks across sectors and geographies,
  • Contagion channels in funding, derivatives and collateral networks,
  • Slow-burning structural risks such as climate, nature and transition,
  • Macro-economic and technological shifts that reprice entire portfolios.

In that sense, systemic solvency sits at the crossroads of microprudential solvency, financial stability, and macroprudential stress-testing, extending existing toolkits to longer horizons and wider system boundaries.

2. Why a dedicated banner like SystemicSolvency.com matters

As systemic risk agendas expand (climate, nature, energy, technology, geopolitics), central banks and supervisors face a communications challenge:

  • Explain complex system-wide work programmes in plain language,
  • Differentiate between entity-level solvency and systemic solvency,
  • Anchor long-horizon policy work that extends beyond usual cycles,
  • Engage governments, markets and the public around a coherent narrative.

A neutral, descriptive asset such as SystemicSolvency.com can provide:

  • A single front door for systemic solvency frameworks, dashboards and reports,
  • A stable label above evolving models, datasets and scenarios,
  • A way to connect planetary boundaries and financial stability under one heading.
